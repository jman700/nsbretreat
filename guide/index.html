The grill runs on the home's natural gas line — no propane tank to deal with. Follow these steps:

  1. Find the mechanical gas timer to the left of the grill behind the small stainless steel square access door. Open the door and twist the timer to start gas flow.
  2. Inside the cabinet under the grill, confirm the shut-off knob is in-line with the hose (gas on). If the knob is turned sideways, gas is off and the grill won't light.
  3. Light and use the grill as normal.
  4. When the timer runs out, gas shuts off automatically. For extended grilling sessions, simply twist the timer again to add more time.
  5. When finished, turn the timer off to stop gas flow.

Hot Tub

The hot tub, bubbles, and pool/spa lights are all controlled from the tablet mounted on the wall in the kitchen. Each function runs on a 3-hour timer — reactivate it if you need longer. Want it pre-heated before you arrive? Just message us and we'll set it up.

  1. 1
    Touch the tablet screen to wake it, then slide your finger upward from the bottom to unlock.
  2. 2
    Tap "815 NSB Pool System" to open controls.
  3. 3
    Select the "OneTouch Scenes" tab and toggle on "Spa Mode." The system will begin heating after a few minutes.
  4. 4
    Allow approximately 30 minutes to reach 102°F depending on starting temperature and weather.

Important: Under the Devices section, "Spa Mode" must be toggled on — not just "Spa Heater." Using only "Spa Heater" will not heat correctly.

On the tablet: select the "Equipment" tab, then toggle on "Air Blower."

On the tablet: select the "Equipment" tab, then toggle on "Pool Light."

When finished, please turn off heating to avoid unnecessary gas usage: select the "OneTouch Scenes" tab and toggle off "Spa Mode."

The sliding glass doors to the pool have a legally required audible alarm — a grey rectangular box to the left of the door. Please confirm it is turned on when you arrive.

  • To exit: Press the mute button before opening the door.
  • To re-enter: Press the small button on the outside of the door frame before opening.
  • The mute lasts 10–15 seconds — if the door stays open longer, the alarm will sound.
  • If your group is adults only, you may disable the alarm using the black switch on the left side of the device. Please do not disable if children are present.

Beach Rules & Tips

Follow these rules provided by Volusia County to avoid getting in trouble while on the beach.

  • Always swim near a lifeguard tower and do not go past waist deep waters.
  • The 10 mph speed limit is strictly enforced. Violators will be fined $116 and up.
  • Alcohol is not allowed on the beach. Pour drinks into cups before heading down to avoid any trouble.
  • Glass containers are strictly prohibited. Single-use plastics (like Solo Cups) are allowed. Reusable plastic or insulated cups are even better.
  • Pets are not allowed on the beach, with the exception of service animals as governed by the Americans with Disabilities Act. Dog-friendly beach areas do exist, such as parts of Smyrna Dunes Park.
  • Music or noise heard from more than 50 feet away is illegal.
  • It is illegal to disturb dune vegetation.
  • Driving or parking in the conservation zone is prohibited (marked by poles stating no parking beyond that point).
  • Beachgoers may grill on the beach. If using charcoal, you must take it with you when leaving — charcoal cannot be disposed of in beach trash/recycle containers or left in the sand.
  • NSB is a sea turtle nesting area — do not disturb nests or use bright lights near the beach after dark.
  • Beach gear is in the garage — chairs, umbrellas, towels, a utility cart, toys, and sporting equipment. Please clean and return everything after use so the next guests can enjoy them too.
  • Bikes are in the garage — lock code is 81518. Biking or walking to the beach is strongly recommended during peak season when parking fills up fast.
  • Get there early on holidays. The beach can reach vehicle capacity quickly and access ramps may close until vehicles leave.
  • Bring shade. We provide a few umbrellas and a canopy in the garage. If you need an upgrade, there's an umbrella and chair rental company near the large yellow lifeguard tower on the beach.
  • Grilling on the beach is a fun and cost-effective option. Remember to take your charcoal back with you. Alternatively, food trucks are usually near the Flagler Avenue entrance, or order delivery and meet the driver at the nearest beach access point.
  • The car wash bucket in the garage isn't for your car — it makes a great hand-washing bucket at the beach. Fill it from the outdoor shower to rinse sand off your hands before eating.
  • Florida sun is intense — reapply sunscreen every 90 minutes.
  • Always check surf and weather conditions before entering the water. See the Current Beach Conditions card at the top of the guide.
